Techniques

Master These 9 Study Techniques

01
Pomodoro Technique

Work in 25-minute bursts, break for 5 minutes. After 4 rounds take a 20-minute break. Keeps your brain fresh and maintains razor-sharp focus throughout the day.

02
Spaced Repetition

Review material at increasing intervals — 1 day, 3 days, 1 week, 1 month. This uses the spacing effect to dramatically boost long-term retention without extra effort.

03
Active Recall

Close your notes and test yourself from memory. This forces your brain to retrieve information rather than passively re-read it — far more effective for retention.

04
Feynman Technique

Explain a concept as if teaching a 12-year-old. If you struggle to simplify it, you've found a gap. Go back, study that gap, and explain it again until it's crystal clear.

05
Interleaved Practice

Mix different subjects or problem types in one session instead of blocking. This strengthens your ability to distinguish concepts and apply knowledge flexibly in exams.

06
Sleep & Memory

During sleep, your hippocampus consolidates memories into long-term storage. 7–8 hours is non-negotiable, especially before exams. All-nighters sabotage retention.

07
Mind Mapping

Create visual diagrams connecting key ideas. Mind maps engage both hemispheres of the brain, making complex interconnected topics easier to understand and remember.

08
Eliminate Distractions

Phone in another room, website blocker on. Research shows a single distraction can cost up to 23 minutes of focused work. A clean environment doubles your output.

09
Cornell Note Method

Divide pages into cue, notes, and summary sections. After class, write questions in the cue column and test yourself. This turns passive notes into an active study tool.
